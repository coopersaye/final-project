1990s Video Games

The 1990s was a transformative decade that saw video games evolve from 2D to 3D graphics, marking a significant leap in gaming technology and storytelling capabilities.

Key Consoles

  • Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) - 16-bit powerhouse with advanced graphics and sound
  • Sega Genesis - Competed fiercely with Nintendo in the "console wars"
  • PlayStation - Sony's entry revolutionized gaming with CD-based games
  • Nintendo 64 - Brought 3D gaming to the mainstream

Iconic Games

  • Super Mario 64 (1996) - Pioneered 3D platforming and camera controls
  • The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998) - Considered one of the greatest games of all time
  • Final Fantasy VII (1997) - Brought JRPGs to Western audiences with cinematic storytelling
  • Doom (1993) - Popularized the first-person shooter genre
  • Street Fighter II (1991) - Defined the fighting game genre
  • Sonic the Hedgehog (1991) - Sega's mascot and answer to Mario

Notable Achievements

The 1990s introduced 3D graphics, CD-ROM technology, and more complex narratives. The console wars between Nintendo and Sega, and later Sony, drove innovation and competition. This decade also saw the rise of PC gaming and the birth of online multiplayer gaming.
