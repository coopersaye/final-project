2000s Video Games

The 2000s brought video games into the mainstream, with online gaming, improved graphics, and the emergence of new genres that would define modern gaming culture.

Key Consoles

  • PlayStation 2 - Became the best-selling console of all time
  • Xbox - Microsoft's entry into console gaming with built-in online capabilities
  • Nintendo GameCube - Nintendo's sixth-generation console
  • Xbox 360 - Introduced achievements and online gaming services
  • PlayStation 3 - Featured Blu-ray and advanced graphics
  • Nintendo Wii - Revolutionized gaming with motion controls

Iconic Games

  • Grand Theft Auto III (2001) - Popularized open-world gaming
  • Halo: Combat Evolved (2001) - Defined console first-person shooters
  • World of Warcraft (2004) - Brought MMORPGs to mainstream audiences
  • Half-Life 2 (2004) - Showcased advanced physics and storytelling
  • Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare (2007) - Set new standards for online multiplayer
  • Wii Sports (2006) - Made gaming accessible to casual audiences

Notable Achievements

The 2000s saw the rise of online gaming, digital distribution, and HD graphics. Motion controls expanded the gaming audience, while online multiplayer became a standard feature. This decade also marked the beginning of mobile gaming and indie game development gaining recognition.
